Technical FAQ Questions and Answers

Here are 40 technical FAQ questions and answers to help you achieve final mastery in chartered accountancy: 1. What is the difference between IFRS and GAAP? IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards) is a set of accounting standards used globally, while GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles) is a set of accounting standards used in a specific country or region. IFRS is more widely used and accepted, while GAAP is used in countries like the United States. 2. How do you calculate depreciation using the straight-line method? Depreciation using the straight-line method is calculated by subtracting the residual value from the cost of the asset and dividing the result by the useful life of the asset. 3. What is the purpose of a audit committee? The purpose of an audit committee is to oversee the financial reporting process and ensure that the financial statements are accurate and reliable. 4. How do you account for goodwill in a business combination? Goodwill is accounted for by recognizing it as an intangible asset on the balance sheet, and amortizing it over its useful life. 5. What is the difference between a current asset and a non-current asset? A current asset is an asset that is expected to be converted into cash within one year or within the company's normal operating cycle, while a non-current asset is an asset that is not expected to be converted into cash within one year or within the company's normal operating cycle. 6. How do you calculate the cost of goods sold? The cost of goods sold is calculated by adding the beginning inventory, purchases, and cost of labor and overhead, and subtracting the ending inventory. 7. What is the purpose of a budget? The purpose of a budget is to provide a financial plan for the company, and to help management make informed decisions about how to allocate resources. 8. How do you account for a lease under IFRS 16? A lease under IFRS 16 is accounted for by recognizing a right-of-use asset and a lease liability on the balance sheet, and amortizing the right-of-use asset over the lease term. 9. What is the difference between a financial asset and a non-financial asset? A financial asset is an asset that is a contractual right to receive cash or another financial asset, while a non-financial asset is an asset that is not a contractual right to receive cash or another financial asset. 10. How do you calculate the weighted average cost of capital? The weighted average cost of capital is calculated by multiplying the cost of each source of capital by its proportion of the total capital, and summing the results. 11. What is the purpose of a financial statement analysis? The purpose of a financial statement analysis is to provide stakeholders with a comprehensive understanding of a company's financial performance and position. 12. How do you account for a business combination under IFRS 3? A business combination under IFRS 3 is accounted for by recognizing the acquiree's assets and liabilities at fair value, and recognizing goodwill as an intangible asset. 13. What is the difference between a defined contribution plan and a defined benefit plan? A defined contribution plan is a pension plan in which the employer contributes a fixed amount to the employee's pension account, while a defined benefit plan is a pension plan in which the employer promises to pay a fixed benefit to the employee in retirement. 14. How do you calculate the present value of a future cash flow? The present value of a future cash flow is calculated by discounting the cash flow using a discount rate, such as the weighted average cost of capital. 15. What is the purpose of a risk management system? The purpose of a risk management system is to identify, assess, and mitigate risks that could impact the company's financial performance and position. 16. How do you account for a foreign currency transaction?
