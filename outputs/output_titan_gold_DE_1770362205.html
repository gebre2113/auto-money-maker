Types of ROI

There are several types of ROI, each with its unique characteristics and applications. As a Wealth Engineer, it's essential to understand these differences to make informed decisions:

  • Financial ROI: Measures the return on investment in terms of financial gains, such as revenue growth or cost savings.
  • Operational ROI: Evaluates the return on investment in terms of improved efficiency, productivity, or quality.
  • Strategic ROI: Assesses the return on investment in terms of long-term goals, such as market share or competitive advantage.

Calculating ROI

The formula for calculating ROI is straightforward:

ROI = (Gain from Investment - Cost of Investment) / Cost of Investment

However, this formula can be modified to suit specific needs, such as:

  • Net Present Value (NPV) ROI: Takes into account the time value of money and calculates the present value of future cash flows.
  • Internal Rate of Return (IRR) ROI: Calculates the rate at which the investment's cash inflows equal its cash outflows.
ROI Analysis Tools

There are numerous tools available to help you analyze and optimize your ROI. As a Wealth Engineer, it's essential to familiarize yourself with these tools:
